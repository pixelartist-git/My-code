Understanding Diabetes: Causes, Symptoms, and Prevention

Date Published: November 15, 2024

Author: Min Thuta Kyaw

Diabetes is a chronic health condition that affects how your body turns food into energy. It occurs when the body cannot properly produce or use insulin, a hormone that regulates blood sugar levels. There are two main types of diabetes: Type 1, where the body does not produce insulin, and Type 2, where the body does not use insulin effectively.

Common symptoms of diabetes include increased thirst, frequent urination, extreme fatigue, and blurred vision. If left untreated, diabetes can lead to serious health complications, such as heart disease, kidney damage, and nerve problems.

Preventing Type 2 diabetes involves maintaining a healthy lifestyle. This includes eating a balanced diet, exercising regularly, managing stress, and maintaining a healthy weight. For those with diabetes, effective management includes monitoring blood sugar levels, taking prescribed medications, and following a diabetes care plan developed with a healthcare professional.

By understanding diabetes and its risks, you can take steps to prevent or manage this condition effectively, ensuring a healthier and more active life.
