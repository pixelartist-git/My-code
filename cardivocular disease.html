Cardiovascular Disease: The Most Common Type of NCD, Explained

Date Published: November 15, 2024

Author: Min Thuta Kyaw

Cardiovascular disease (CVD) is a group of problems that affect the heart and blood vessels. It is the most common type of non-communicable disease (NCD), which means it is not passed from person to person. CVD includes conditions like heart attacks, strokes, and high blood pressure. These diseases happen when the blood vessels become blocked or damaged, making it hard for the heart to pump blood.

Several factors can increase the risk of developing cardiovascular disease. These include smoking, being overweight, not exercising, eating unhealthy foods, and having a family history of heart problems. Stress and drinking too much alcohol can also contribute to CVD.
